Astanga yoga

That one word says so much to anyone who knows how to listen. It carries within it the power of the infinite path and experience created over thousands of years. Ancient wisdom and practice, which was once a feature of the East, today is scattered all over the world, in the most remote parts of our colorful planet, and thus offers man the answer to eternal questions.

Rate: 3

Difficulty: 3

Duration: 50



Karma yoga

Karma yoga deals with cause, action and consequence through selfless service to others. The word karma means to do, to act. Every activity of ours - breathing, speech, movement, but also every thought and every word - is all karma, sharing. However, the term karma also means the universal law of cause and effect.

Rate: 2

Difficulty: 4

Duration: 45



Kings yoga

aja yoga (translated as royal yoga) is a spiritual path that leads to realization, through discipline, self-restraint and uncompromising control of the mind. The nature of the mind (meaning the conscious, subconscious and unconscious part) is considered an obstacle in the form of circular formations (vritia) like concentric waves that form when we throw a stone into the water, and prevents Consciousness (Purusha) from being in all its radiance and purity. manifests through the mind-body instrument (Prakriti)

Rate: 5

Difficulty: 5

Duration: 45

Prental yoga

Prenatal yoga refers to all yoga exercises that are practiced during pregnancy. The word yoga means integrity and unification. In other words, through the practice of yoga we strive to establish mental, physical and spiritual wholeness with ourselves, but also with the whole world around us. Prenatal yoga, in addition to all the above, creates a special connection between the mother and the child who grows up in her, and prepares the woman completely for childbirth and all the challenges that motherhood brings with it.

Rate: 4

Difficulty: 5

Duration: 60
